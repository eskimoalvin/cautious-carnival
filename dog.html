Playing With My Dog

About My Dog

I am the proud owner of a 4 year old puppy. His name is Biggie Paws Notorious D.O.G.

He is an Australian Shepherd, Blue Heeler, Border Collie mix. Although he is somewhat of a mutt, we love him all the same.

All Three of the breeds that make him up are very rambunctious breeds. They were all made for herding farm animals.

This ensures that he always has quite a bit of extra energy. This is fine for when that type of dog works on a farm, ours, however, does not.

This means that in order to burn off all of that extra energy that he was graced with, we need to spend our free time working him out.

Some of the things that we do to help him burn off that energy every day are:

  • 1 hour of playtime at the local dog park
  • around 30 minutes of "brain games"
  • Some kind of walk usually totalling 45 minutes
  • lot of belly rubs!

These are things that we do every day to ensure that he does not let his energy out in unconstructive ways.

Shortly after we got him, we were unaware of the challenges of owning a working dog.

We found out one day when my wife came downstairs to a ruined couch. We got him a trainer who taught us how to handle our little bundle of crazy shortly after that incident.

We love Biggie very much despite all the work that he presents. He is a very well behaved dog who loves us and would do anything to keep us safe.
